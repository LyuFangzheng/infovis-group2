    An application for identifying toxic subreddits

    Due to the frequent use of social media, people interact more and more on platforms in which they create, share, and/or exchange information. Some of this interactions may be negative and are intended to harm or at least discomfort one's audience, which is also known as trolling. In this application we will focus on a popular social media platform called reddit.com which hosts a large number of sub-communities called subreddits. Various subreddits have particular topics and people can share their opinion. As you can imagine, lots of these opinions might be negative. This application is designed for a reddit moderator to identify these toxic subreddits by visualising the influences they have on each other.

    The following visualisations will illustrate the toxic interactions between the subreddits.

    • Graph The graph will give an overview of the connections. The user can zoom in on clusters to better see the influences of individual subreddits. The user can choose what the edges will show: intensity, positive relation or negative relation. The dropdown menu can be used to select a layer of clusters. On the deepest level, where the nodes represent individual subreddits, it can also be chosen whether the edges represent weights or sentiment. By hovering a node all links of the node will appear. If a node in layer 2 (subreddit level) is clicked on, the name of the subreddit will be shown in the text block above the graph. The time range of the information that the graph is showing can be altered by resizing and moving the timeslider.
    • Heatmap The heatmap also presents information from the selected timeframe. It shows the sentiment of interactions between subreddits, with interactions going from left (y-axis) to top (x-axis). The more red a square, the more negative an interaction, and the greener, the more positive.
    • Barchart The barchart shows semantic information about selected subreddits, using Linguistic Inquiry and Word Count (LIWC) scores.

    Data was gathered from here. In the data set, 55,863 subreddits (nodes) and 858,490 hyperlinks between subreddits (edges) are present.
